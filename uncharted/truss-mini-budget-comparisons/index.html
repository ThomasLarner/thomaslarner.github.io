Just How Radical Was The Truss Budget

How it stacks up to previous budget announcements.

Sep 30, 2022

Truss’ mini budget was in fact one of the biggest in recent history. Tax cuts totalled more than £146bn and were at levels not seen since the 70’s (with respect to GDP). Borrowing announcements included an extra £72.4bn, to take the DMO’s total NFR¹ to £234.1bn for 2022-23. This is extra borrowing at quantities seen in budgets during the 2008 Financial Crisis.

But the underlying theme in recent budgets is the extremity of the announcements. 2020 onwards have seen some of the most extreme budgets in recent history.

When will politicians end this era of fiscal extremism, and if the most recent budget is anything to go by – how much more can markets take.

¹NFR - Net financing agreement
